You do not need a full architectural set to begin, but coming in with a few dimensions, reference photos, and a rough wish list gives you a much better starting point.

The most helpful first appointments are the ones where we can quickly understand your space, your style, and your priorities. Even a simple folder on your phone can be enough if it covers the basics.

## Start With Your Space

Bring rough room dimensions, appliance widths, and any information you already have about plumbing, windows, or soffits. Perfect measurements are not required on day one, but approximate dimensions help narrow the right cabinet lines and layout directions.

## Save Visual References

Photos are one of the fastest ways to communicate what you like.

- Save a few kitchens or bathrooms you keep coming back to.
- Note whether you are reacting to the cabinet color, door profile, countertop, or overall mood.
- Include a couple of examples you do **not** like so the conversation is more precise.

## Make a Priority List

### Helpful categories

1. Layout and storage
2. Cabinet style and finish
3. Countertop durability
4. Timeline and budget

If you already know that easy maintenance matters more than dramatic veining, or that you need deeper drawers for cookware, say that early. Those details shape recommendations quickly.

## Bring Questions

The first appointment is the right time to ask about lead times, installation flow, finish differences, and tradeoffs between material options. The clearer the questions, the easier it is to map out practical next steps.
