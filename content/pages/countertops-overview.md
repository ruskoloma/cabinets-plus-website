          - question: Are your countertops seamless in design?
            answer: 'Seamless countertops depend on the size of your kitchen or bathroom and the layout of your cabinets. Slabs are typically up to 126" long and 63" wide. For spaces within that limit you can enjoy a truly seamless look — otherwise, we''ll join slabs carefully for a clean appearance.'
          - question: Can I use my countertops as a cutting surface?
            answer: 'We recommend using a cutting board to protect both your knives and your countertop. Granite and quartzite resist scratches well, but any natural or engineered surface can dull or chip a blade over time.'
          - question: How do I care for my new countertops?
            answer: 'Quartz only needs soap and water. Natural stones like granite, marble, and quartzite should be sealed periodically and cleaned with a pH-neutral stone cleaner to keep them looking their best.'
          - question: Can I see samples of your countertop options before making a decision?
            answer: Yes — visit our Spokane showroom to see and feel full slabs and material samples. Our team can help you pick the best fit for your kitchen or bathroom project.
          - question: How long does it take to install new countertops?
            answer: Most installs take 1–2 days after fabrication. Lead time from template to install typically ranges from 1 to 3 weeks depending on material availability and project size.
          - question: How much does it cost to install new countertops?
            answer: 'Pricing depends on material, slab size, and edge profiles. We provide detailed quotes during your consultation after we review your measurements and design.'
          - question: Do you handle sink cutouts and edge profiles?
            answer: 'Yes. We fabricate sink cutouts, cooktop openings, and a full range of edge profiles including straight, eased, beveled, bullnose, and ogee.'
          - question: Do you offer a warranty on countertops?
